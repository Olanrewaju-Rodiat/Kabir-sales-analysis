# Kabir-sales-analysis
 Executive Summary
Sales performance shows a consistent decline over the three-year period, with total quantity sold dropping from 476 in 2010 to 368 in 2012. This downward trend indicates weakening demand and reduced sales productivity. Overall, the business is experiencing declining performance and requires strategic improvements to restore growth and efficiency.
 Business Recommendations
1. Sales Representative with Highest Transaction but Not Highest Revenue
Abdul recorded one of the highest transaction counts among the sales representatives but did not generate the highest revenue.
While Tola had the highest number of transactions overall (376) and also the highest revenue, Abdul stands out as a representative with high transaction volume (277) but comparatively lower total revenue (60,940) than Tola (82,720).
2. Sales Behaviour Insight (Trend Analysis)
Abdul’s performance suggests a high-volume, low-value sales pattern.
This indicates that:
He likely sells lower-priced products, or
Handles smaller transaction sizes, or
Applies discount-driven sales strategies
In contrast, Tola’s performance reflects a high-value sales approach, generating more revenue with strong transaction volume.
Insight:
The company should encourage strategies that balance volume and value, focusing not just on the number of sales but also on revenue per transaction.
3. Yearly Performance Analysis
Total quantity sold per year:
2010: 476
2011: 380
2012: 368
This shows a consistent decline in sales performance over the three-year period.
4. Recommendation for 2013 (Hiring Decision)
Based on the observed trend, the company should not hire additional staff in 2013.
Reasoning:
Sales volume is decreasing year-on-year
Hiring more staff may lead to:
Increased operational costs
Reduced productivity per employee
Recommendation: Instead of expanding the workforce, the company should:
Improve sales strategies
Provide training for existing staff
Focus on high-value transactions
Analyze causes of declining sales and address them
